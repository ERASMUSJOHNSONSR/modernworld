Opening Images

Before you can view or edit a photo, you need to open it in an image viewer or editor. This is usually done by selecting the file.

No image opened yet.

Ready to open a photo?

Click "Simulate Open" to see how it works.

Step 1: Locate Image File

Find the image file (e.g., .jpg, .png) on your computer, usually in your "Pictures" folder.

Step 2: Double-Click or Open With

Double-click the image file. It will typically open in your default photo viewer. Or, right-click and choose "Open with..." to select a specific program.

Result: Image Displayed

The image is now visible in your photo viewer or editing software, ready for viewing or editing.

Basic Cropping

Cropping allows you to remove unwanted parts of an image, focusing on a specific area. It's like cutting out a section of a physical photo. Click "Simulate Crop" to see the selection area.

Crop Your Photo:
