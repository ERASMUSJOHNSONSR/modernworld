Viruses and Malware: Digital Threats

"Malware" is a broad term for malicious software. Viruses are a specific type of malware. Click to learn more about each.

What is a Virus? ‚ñº

A **computer virus** is a type of malicious software that attaches itself to another program (like a document or application) and, when that program is run, replicates itself by modifying other computer programs and inserting its own code.

Viruses often spread from one computer to another through infected files or removable media.

What is Malware? ‚ñº

**Malware** is a general term for any software designed to intentionally cause damage to a computer, server, client, or computer network, or to steal data.

Common types include:

  • **Spyware:** Secretly monitors your computer activity.
  • **Adware:** Displays unwanted advertisements.
  • **Ransomware:** Encrypts your files and demands payment.
  • **Worms:** Self-replicating malware that spreads independently across networks.
  • **Trojans:** Disguises itself as legitimate software to gain access.

Phishing: Tricking You Online

Phishing is a type of cyberattack that uses deceptive emails, websites, or messages to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like usernames, passwords, and credit card details. Click on the "red flags" in the simulated email to learn how to spot them.

Phishing Red Flags Explained:

Click on the highlighted "red flags" in the email to understand common phishing tactics.

**Suspicious Sender:** The email address looks like a legitimate company, but it's slightly off or from a generic domain. Always check the full email address, not just the display name.

**Urgent/Threatening Subject:** Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency or fear to pressure you into acting quickly without thinking.

**Suspicious Link:** The text of the link might look legitimate, but the actual URL it points to (which you can see by hovering over it) is different or suspicious. Never click links from unexpected or suspicious emails.

Antivirus Software: Your Digital Guardian

Antivirus (AV) software is designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software. It acts as a shield for your computer.

How Antivirus Works:

  • **Scanning:** Regularly checks your files and programs for known malware signatures.
  • **Detection:** Identifies suspicious behavior or code patterns.
  • **Removal/Quarantine:** If malware is found, it attempts to remove it or isolate it in a safe area (quarantine).
  • **Real-time Protection:** Monitors your computer continuously for new threats.
  • **Updates:** Needs regular updates to recognize the latest threats.

Key Takeaways for Internet Security

By following these simple practices, you can significantly improve your online safety.

  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: For every online account. Consider a password manager.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.
  • Keep Software Updated: Operating system, browser, and all applications. Updates often include security patches.
  • Be Wary of Suspicious Emails/Links: "When in doubt, throw it out!" Verify senders and hover over links.
  • Install and Update Antivirus Software: A reliable AV program is your first line of defense.
  • Backup Your Data: Regularly back up important files to an external drive or cloud service.
  • Use a Firewall: Most operating systems have one built-in; ensure it's active.
