The Alternatives

Beyond a full shutdown, these options offer convenience for different situations. Click each tab to learn about its purpose, pros, and cons.

Sleep Mode

A low-power state that keeps your session in RAM for a quick resume. Ideal for short breaks.

Pros

  • Wakes up almost instantly
  • Resume exactly where you left off

Cons

  • Uses a small amount of power
  • Session is lost if power is cut

Hibernate Mode

Saves your session to the hard drive and powers off completely. Best for long periods away from a power source.

Pros

  • Uses zero power
  • Your session is safe without power

Cons

  • Slower to resume than sleep

Restart (Reboot)

Shuts the computer down and immediately starts it again. It's the classic fix for many common problems.

Why Use It?

  • Clears temporary memory (RAM)
  • Fixes sluggishness or glitches
  • Applies system updates
  • Fundamental troubleshooting step

When to Use It

Use a restart when your computer is acting strangely, running slow, or after installing new software or updates.
