Managing MordernWorld Digital Content

Learn the essential skills for organizing your computer: creating new folders, renaming files and folders, deleting unwanted items, and understanding the Recycle Bin/Trash.

Creating New Folders

Organize your files by creating new folders. Follow these steps to make a new container for your digital items.

New Folder

Ready to create a folder?

Click "Next Step" to begin the process.

Step 1: Choose Location

Navigate to the location where you want to create the new folder (e.g., your Documents folder, or the Desktop).

Step 2: Right-Click

Right-click on an empty space within that location. A context menu will appear.

Step 3: Select "New" then "Folder"

From the context menu, hover over "New" and then click "Folder."

Step 4: Name Your Folder

A new folder icon will appear, typically named "New folder." Type your desired name for the folder and press Enter.

Renaming Files and Folders

Change the name of a file or folder to better reflect its content or purpose. This process is similar for both.

Old Name.txt

Ready to rename?

Click "Next Step" to learn how to rename.

Step 1: Select Item

Click once on the file or folder you wish to rename to select it.

Step 2: Initiate Rename

Right-click the selected item and choose "Rename" from the context menu. Alternatively, click the item once to select it, then click it again (slowly, not a double-click) to enter rename mode.

Step 3: Type New Name

The current name will be highlighted. Type the new name for your file or folder.

Step 4: Confirm

Press Enter or click anywhere outside the name field to confirm the new name. The item will now have its updated name.

Deleting Files and Folders

Remove unwanted files or folders from your computer. Deleted items usually go to a temporary holding area.

Ready to delete?

Click "Next Step" to learn how to delete items.

Step 1: Select Item(s)

Click on the file or folder (or multiple items) you want to delete.

Step 2: Delete Action

Press the Delete key on your keyboard, or right-click the item and choose "Delete" from the context menu.

Step 3: Confirm (if prompted)

You might be asked to confirm the deletion. Click "Yes" or "OK" to proceed. The item will move to the Recycle Bin/Trash.

Using the Recycle Bin / Trash

The Recycle Bin (Windows) or Trash (macOS) is a temporary storage area for deleted files and folders, allowing you to recover them if needed.

Your Digital Safety Net

Items deleted from your computer are usually sent here, not immediately erased. This gives you a chance to recover them.

Key Actions:

  • **Open:** Double-click the Recycle Bin/Trash icon on your desktop to view its contents.
  • **Restore:** Right-click an item inside and choose "Restore" to send it back to its original location.
  • **Empty:** Right-click the Recycle Bin/Trash icon and choose "Empty Recycle Bin" / "Empty Trash" to permanently delete all contents. Be sure you no longer need these files!
  • **Shift + Delete (Windows):** Pressing Shift + Delete bypasses the Recycle Bin and permanently deletes files immediately. Use with caution!
