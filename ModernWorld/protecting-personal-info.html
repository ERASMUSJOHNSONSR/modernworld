What Information Should You Share Online?

Not all information is created equal. Be mindful of what you post or provide on websites and social media.

Generally Safe to Share (with caution) ‚ñº

This information is often public or necessary for certain online interactions, but still requires discretion:

  • **Public Name:** Your first name or a chosen username for public profiles.
  • **General Interests:** Hobbies, favorite books, movies (on social media).
  • **Non-Specific Location:** Your city or general region, not your exact address.
  • **Public Opinions:** Your thoughts on non-sensitive topics.

Always remember: Once it's online, it's hard to take back. Think before you post!

Be Very Cautious (Share only when necessary and trusted) ‚ñº

This information can be used to identify or target you. Share only with trusted services and when explicitly required:

  • **Full Name:** On professional sites or for official transactions.
  • **Email Address:** For account creation or trusted newsletters. Expect some spam.
  • **Phone Number:** For two-factor authentication or verified contacts.
  • **Date of Birth:** Often used for identity verification; share minimally.
  • **Photos:** Be mindful of what's in the background, who else is in the photo, and location tags.

Verify the legitimacy of any request for this information before providing it.

Never Share (Keep private at all costs) ‚ñº

This information is highly sensitive and can lead to identity theft, financial fraud, or other serious security risks. Never share it publicly or with unverified sources:

  • **Passwords:** Never share your passwords with anyone, ever.
  • **Bank Account Numbers / Credit Card Details:** Only enter these on secure, trusted payment pages.
  • **Social Security Number (or national ID equivalent):** Highly sensitive, rarely needed online.
  • **Exact Home Address:** Avoid sharing publicly.
  • **Private Health Information:** Keep medical details strictly confidential.
  • **Login Credentials:** For any online service.

Scammers actively try to trick you into revealing these details. Be extremely vigilant.

Recognizing Secure Sites (HTTPS)

When you share personal or financial information online, it's crucial to ensure the website is secure. Look for these indicators in your browser's address bar.

What Makes a Site Secure?

Click on the "https://" or the padlock icon in the address bar to learn about secure websites.

**HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure):** This means the connection between your browser and the website is encrypted. Any data you send (like passwords or credit card numbers) is scrambled, making it unreadable to anyone trying to intercept it.

**Padlock Icon:** A closed padlock icon (üîí) in the address bar is a visual indicator that the site is using HTTPS and has a valid security certificate. It confirms that the connection is secure and the site's identity has been verified.

Key Takeaways for Protecting Your Information

Be proactive and vigilant about your online privacy and security.

  • Think Before You Share: Assume anything you post online could become public and permanent.
  • Verify Website Security: Always check for "https://" and the padlock icon before entering sensitive information.
  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: For every account.
  • Be Wary of Phishing: Don't click suspicious links or open unexpected attachments.
  • Keep Software Updated: Operating systems, browsers, and apps often release security patches.
  • Review Privacy Settings: On social media and other online services, regularly check and adjust your privacy settings.
