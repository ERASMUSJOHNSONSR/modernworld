Creating Strong Passwords

A strong password is long, complex, and unpredictable. It makes it much harder for attackers to guess or crack it.

Strength: Too Short

What Makes a Password Strong?

  • **Length:** Aim for at least 12-16 characters. Longer is always better.
  • **Complexity:** Mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • **Unpredictability:** Avoid common words, personal information, or sequential patterns.
  • **Uniqueness:** Never reuse passwords across different accounts.

Importance of Unique Passwords

Reusing passwords is a major security risk. If one account is compromised, all accounts using that same password become vulnerable to "credential stuffing" attacks.

Key Takeaways for Password Management

Protecting your online identity starts with smart password habits.

  • Aim for Length: The longer your password, the harder it is to crack. Passphrases (e.g., "CorrectHorseBatteryStaple") are excellent.
  • Mix It Up: Combine uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Be Unique: Use a different, strong password for every single online account.
  • Consider a Password Manager: These tools securely store and generate complex, unique passwords for you, so you only need to remember one master password.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Even with strong passwords, 2FA adds an extra layer of security, requiring a second verification step (like a code from your phone).
  • Avoid Personal Information: Don't use your name, birthdate, pet's name, or other easily guessable details.
