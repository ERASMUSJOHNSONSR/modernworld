What is Email?

Email (electronic mail) is a method of exchanging digital messages over the Internet. It's like sending a letter, but instantly and digitally.

Understanding Spam: Stay Safe Online

Spam refers to unsolicited, unwanted, or junk email messages. It can be annoying and sometimes dangerous. Learn how to protect yourself.

What is Spam? ‚ñº

Spam is typically commercial in nature (ads, promotions) but can also be phishing attempts (trying to trick you into revealing personal info) or malware distribution.

Most email providers use filters to automatically move suspected spam to a "Junk" or "Spam" folder, but some might still get through.

How to Identify and Avoid Spam ‚ñº
Common Red Flags:
  • **Suspicious Sender:** Unfamiliar email addresses, generic names, or addresses that don't match the company they claim to be from.
  • **Generic Greetings:** "Dear Customer" instead of your name.
  • **Urgent/Threatening Language:** Messages demanding immediate action or threatening consequences.
  • **Too Good to Be True Offers:** "You've won a lottery!" or unbelievable discounts.
  • **Poor Grammar/Spelling:** Many spam emails contain obvious errors.
  • **Suspicious Links/Attachments:** Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. Never open unexpected attachments.
Best Practices:
  • **Don't Reply:** Replying confirms your email is active.
  • **Don't Click Links:** Especially if suspicious.
  • **Mark as Spam:** Use your email client's "Mark as Spam" or "Junk" button to help train your filter.
  • **Delete Suspicious Emails:** When in doubt, delete it.
  • **Be Cautious with Personal Info:** Never give out passwords, bank details, or sensitive information via email unless you initiated the contact and are certain of the recipient's identity.
